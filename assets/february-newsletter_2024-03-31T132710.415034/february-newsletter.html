4th NMCC (NATIONAL MOOT COURT COMPETITION) 
School of Law

 

8-10 FEBRUARY, 2024 | SEMINAR HALL, B BLOCK | 8:30 AM - 4:30 PM
 

The School of Law will organise the prestigious national moot court competition, the 4th NMCC, from 8-10 February, 2024. The competition will feature a rigorous format, commencing with a memorial qualifications round and progressing through prelims, quarter-finals, semi-finals, and culminating in the Finals. The moot problem delves into the intricate intersections of Information Technology (IT) and Intellectual Property Rights (IPR) law. This intellectually stimulating competition will provide a platform for legal minds to showcase their prowess and engage in a spirited exchange of legal arguments. 
Join us to navigate the complexities of contemporary legal issues.
 

INTERNATIONAL CONFERENCE ON LITERATURE, DIGITAL POPULAR CULTURE AND THE EVOLVING NARRATIVE

Department of English and Cultural Studies

 

8-9 FEBRUARY, 2024 | HYBRID
 

The Department of English & Cultural Studies will host a conference titled “International Conference On Literature, Digital Popular Culture And The Evolving Narrative” on 8 and 9 February, 2024. 


The speakers of Day 1 are Dr. Sanjukta Poddar (she/her/hers), Assistant Professor, Leiden University, Dr. Abhija Ghosh Assistant Professor, OP Jindal Global University, Dr. Lye Kit Ying, Senior Lecturer, Singapore University of Social Sciences. The Speakers for Day 2 are Dr. Kavya Krishna K. R. Assistant Professor,, IIT (BHU) Varanasi,  Dr. Vipin K Kadavath, Assistant Professor, Banaras Hindu University.
The conference will examine the intricate connections between traditional literary forms, the rise of digital popular culture, and the evolving narrative in today's fast-paced, technology-driven environment.
